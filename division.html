Definition of Division

Let’s say you have 12 donuts and want to distribute them equally among your three friends. How do you decide fairly how much each gets?

Division is the opposite of multiplication. If 3 groups of 4 make 12 in multiplication, 12 divided into 3 equal groups give 4 in each group in division. The purpose of dividing is to see how many equal groups are formed or how many are in each group when sharing equally. Division is meant to split or share amounts between groups.

÷ or /

Division, is commonly represented in two different ways, include, ÷ or /, acting as the symbol of division. In the midst of you solving an equation in a textbook, you are more likely to see this symbol, ÷,

While, you will see, /, in a tech device, since most keyboards have inability to input ÷. Nonetheless, both have the purpose of representing the fourth basic operation, division.

Division Sentence

Like the other operations there are always parts to each opeational sentence, and division is no different. Each division statement, has a dividend, divisor, and quotient.

Each one has a different function, for example, dividend acts as the number divided, while divisor is the number dividing the dividend. Quotient, is result of division.
